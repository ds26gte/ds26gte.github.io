The Underwater Princess

When I was 14, I once went adventuring about in an old abandoned building and found, amidst the rubbish, an old palm-leaf manuscript. After some days consulting at the local library, I was able to decipher the message in it. It was a first-person account of events by a boy, about my age, on the west coast of India, around the beginning of the 16th century. Good evening, fellow Toasmasters and guests, here is the account. I have not added or subtracted any detail:

~

THERE ARE A LOT of ships in our harbor, from up and down the coast, from Ceylon, Indonesia, China, Africa and Arabia. There are dhows, sambuks, junks, carracks, and sampans but lately there has been a new type of ship: the caravel from a land called Portugal. They are like no other ship. They are reputed to have sailed from an entirely different ocean to the west of Africa, after rounding the southern tip of that continent. I didn’t even know that Africa had a southern tip!

The caravels had huge guns, and their sailors have developed quite a reputation for ruthlessness. They have successfully muscled their way against many traditional merchants and local kingdoms. People resented their intrusions but there was nothing they could do about it because they were simply too powerful. I was curious how these ships could have braved the high seas that no one else did. One evening, I swam up to one of them to see for myself what they had. I climbed up the rope ladder and sneaked my way into the hold. There were unimaginable riches there: our local spices of course, but also gems, rubies and sapphires and jade, rich cloths, silks, brocade, and plush carpets, pistols and daggers, and all kinds of instruments such as octants, sextants, astrolabes and floating compasses, and machinery I couldn’t divine the function of. While I was engrossed in admiring what looked like an intricately constructed brass clock, I realized, too late, that I was discovered. Two sailors roughly grabbed me.

"Well, well, well, what kind of sneaking thief we got here?" said one to the other.

"Sir, I’m no thief," I protested. "I was just curious …​"

"Ah, so you’re a spy!" he countered. "It doesn’t matter, the punishment is the same," he cackled.

The punishment, it turned out, was to be shot out of one of their cannons into the Arabian sea. I struggled and pleaded, but there was no escape, and the shore authorities had neither the power not the interest to aid me. Thankfully I fainted as soon as they tucked me into the cannon.

~

WHEN I CAME TO, I found myself drifting through deep ultramarine water. Strangely, I didn’t drown. I felt strong but soft hands guide me through the liquid.

Finally I was dragged onto the edge of an underwater kingdom. My rescuer was a young woman of most unusual aspect. She had shimmering blue and green skin, but only up to her waist. Below she had glittering cerulean blue scales, ending in tail fins.

"Thank you for saving my life," I stuttered, but even as I said it, I wasn’t sure my life was saved, since neither the place nor the woman seemed to be of this world. Perhaps I had died and gone to heaven, or more likely, I was in the antechamber to hell, where things are known to be deceptively comfortable just before one got shoved into a cauldron of boiling oil.

"My pleasure, sir," said the woman, or fish, or mermaid, in a tinkling voice. "I guess I did save you. Perhaps you could do me a favor in return."

Here it comes, I thought, I am going to be carted off to be a slave for her people as they made me do backbreaking work.

"But first you should eat. You seem famished."

Indeed, I was. She led me inland, and with a snap of her fingers, she conjured up a table with exotic fruits and viands arrayed atop it. Small animals hopped in from both the woods and the sea, winked at me cheerfully, and voluntarily cooked themselves on an open fire, before plopping on to my plate.

"Go on," she said.

I tasted the food, including the self-sacrificing animals, and indeed, it was delicious beyond compare. Not knowing what fate was in store for me, I tucked into the food, and when I was done, she sidled up to me and said, "About that favor?"

"Yes, what would you have me do? Clear your impenetrable forests, go into your noxious mines, haul stone to make pyramids? Anything. I owe you my life. Who are you and what is your wish?"

"I am Nina," she said. "I am the princess and heir to the kingdom you are now on. And no, I don’t want you to make pyramids, whatever they are."

"What then, Princess Nina?"

"Ah," she paused, suddenly bashful. "I have never seen such a handsome creature as you. Is there any chance …​ Could you be my prince, and rule this kingdom with me when I become queen?"

I stared dumbfounded. Her face fell.

"Oh, I see I have offended you. I just thought, since you promised …​"

I was, as you can imagine, bewildered. "I’m sorry. Surely you have splendid mermen around who would be a better catch than I." (I bit my tongue when I said the word "catch", but she didn’t seem to notice.)

"Oh the men of my tribe don’t have a leg to stand on," she sighed. I could see her point. I guess it took an alien species like me to sweep her off her feet, I mean, fins.

I needed time to process all this.

She turned to go. "I can see that being with a sea creature like me goes against your moral scruples."

As she was swishing away, I recovered my wits, and called out, "Wait, wait, Nina, um Princess Majesty …​ Yes this isn’t something I would normally do, but I did promise you, and a promise is a promise."

So, saying, in the spirit of selfless self-sacrifice, I held out my arms. We held each other.

I could imagine ruling a peaceful submarine kingdom with her, away from the troubles of my world, even if I had to take a crash course in hydraulic statesmanship.

After a few moments, she looked up into my face. She smiled. I smiled. "Perhaps we should kiss, to seal the treaty of friendship between our peoples," she suggested.

I really didn’t have a cogent argument why not, not that I was trying very hard to formulate one.

"Sure," I said, and leaned in …​ and that’s when I woke up this morning.
